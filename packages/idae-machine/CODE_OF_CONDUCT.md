# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. @medyll/idae-machine is dedicated to building an inclusive environment where all people feel safe to participate, present ideas, and contribute, regardless of:

- Background or identity
- Experience level
- Gender identity or expression
- Age
- Body size
- Ability or disability
- Race or ethnicity
- Religion
- Sexual orientation
- Socioeconomic status
- Or any other dimension of diversity

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Treat all community members with respect and dignity
- **Being inclusive** — Welcome differing opinions and experiences
- **Being constructive** — Provide helpful feedback and engage in good-faith discussion
- **Being patient** — Understand that learning takes time; help others grow
- **Being collaborative** — Work together toward shared goals
- **Using welcoming language** — Avoid gendered pronouns, slurs, or exclusionary language
- **Respecting privacy** — Don't share others' personal information without consent

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** — Including but not limited to offensive comments related to identity, intimidation, threats, or stalking
- **Discrimination** — Treating others unfairly based on any characteristic
- **Unwelcome advances** — Sexual or otherwise
- **Trolling or insulting comments** — Personal attacks or deliberate provocations
- **Sharing private information** — Publishing others' private details without consent
- **Misuse of power** — Using moderator or maintainer privileges to harm others
- **Violation of privacy** — Recording or photographing without consent
- **Sustained disruption** — Deliberate attempts to derail discussions or projects

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community spaces (GitHub issues, discussions, pull requests)
- Official social media accounts
- In-person events and meetings related to the project
- Any space where community members interact on behalf of the project

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. Violations can be reported to:

- **GitHub**: Use the "Report abuse" button on comments or issues
- **Email**: contact the project maintainers directly
- **Maintainers** will review reports confidentially and take appropriate action

### Consequences

Depending on severity, consequences may include:

1. **Warning** — A private message explaining the violation
2. **Temporary suspension** — From participation in community spaces
3. **Permanent removal** — For repeated or severe violations

Appeals of enforcement decisions can be made to the project maintainers.

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Don't engage** in escalating conflict
2. **Document** what happened (dates, messages, witnesses)
3. **Report** to a maintainer or project lead
4. **Follow up** if you don't hear back within 7 days

All reports will be treated seriously and confidentially.
